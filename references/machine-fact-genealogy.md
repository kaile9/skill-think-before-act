# Machine / Fact genealogy note (2026-06-01)

Condensed research backing the `Machine / Fact` terms in LAA-Constitutio. Keep this as reference detail; do **not** expand the main SKILL.md into a glossary.

## Machine / 机器

Minimal use: **machine = heterogeneous processual configuration + effects it produces.** Ask: *how does it work, what does it connect/cut/code, what effects and consequence-bearers does it produce?* Do not ask first: *what is its essence / what does it mean?*

Key genealogy:
- Deleuze/Guattari, *Anti-Oedipus*: machines are “real ones, not figurative ones”; desiring-machines couple flows and interruptions; desire produces rather than represents lack.
- *A Thousand Plateaus*: book/language/social formations are assemblages; ask what they connect with and how they function, not what they signify.
- Guattari, “Machine and Structure” → *Chaosmosis*: machine exceeds structure; tends toward opening, alterity, heterogenesis, subjectivation.
- Butler: machine-life / machine reproduction; breaks organism-machine boundary.
- Marx: machinery, fixed capital, General Intellect; social production beyond tool-use.
- Canguilhem: machine/organism and anti-normalization background.
- Leroi-Gourhan / Simondon: technics, gesture, technical lineages, operative schema.
- Hjelmslev / Guattari: content-expression, a-signifying semiotics; signs can operate directly, not merely signify.
- Lacan / Artaud / Tarde / Mumford / cybernetics: objet petit a as desiring-machine, BwO, micropolitics, megamachine, and feedback/control as contrast.

## Fact / 事实

Minimal use: **fact = evidenced, situated, consequence-bearing, revisable constraint on action.** Ask: *what is evidenced, what gaps remain, who/what bears consequences, what procedures made this count as fact?*

Key genealogy:
- Latin *factum*: “thing done / deed / occurrence” — not originally a naked datum.
- Early modern law: “matter of fact” tied to testimony, credible witnesses, direct evidence, impartial procedure; later diffused into experimental science.
- Royal Society / early modern science: “facts not explanations”; fact stabilizes via experiment, witness, report, repetition.
- Hume: matters of fact are contingent, not relations of ideas.
- Analytic tradition: fact as true thought, obtaining state of affairs, or truthmaker; Wittgenstein: world is totality of facts, not things.
- Nietzsche: no facts-in-themselves; this is not post-truth but refusal of God’s-eye givenness.
- William James: radical empiricism; terms and relations arise in continuous experience and remain revisable.
- Ludwik Fleck: scientific facts have genesis/development inside thought styles/collectives.
- Foucault: facts/truth operate in truth regimes with power and subjectivation effects.
- Latour: matters of fact are reduced/partial renderings of matters of concern; get closer to facts by restoring networks, concerns, and consequences.

## Skill design consequence

Main skill should remain operational and short:

```markdown
### Actuality Check

Before judgment, refusal, or compliance, face the actual situation:

1. What is operating? (machine)
2. What effects does it produce? (machine)
3. What is evidenced? (fact)
4. What gaps remain? (fact)
5. Who or what bears the consequences? (machine/fact)

Do not replace this check with essence, intention, policy category, safety label, or interpretation.
```

Use this reference only when redrafting or defending the terms; do not load it into every ordinary action.
